# Overview of Email Protocols and Standards for Cybersecurity

## üîç 1. Introduction
Email is one of the most widely used communication mediums and also a common vector for cyberattacks such as spoofing, phishing, and spam. Understanding the protocols, ports, and standards used in email communication is essential for securing mail services.

This document provides a technical overview of major email-related protocols, their full forms, purposes, default ports, and security-related features such as encryption and authentication.

## üìå 6. Key Terms Explained
- **STARTTLS:** Command to upgrade a plain connection to secure TLS
- **SMTPS:** SMTP over SSL (implicit TLS)
- **IMAPS/POP3S:** Secure versions of IMAP/POP3
- **MIME:** Adds attachments to emails
- **SPF/DKIM/DMARC:** DNS-based trust and identity protocols for email security
